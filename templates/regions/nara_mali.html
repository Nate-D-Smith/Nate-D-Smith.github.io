Nara, Mali

VEI: 33

Introduction

Nara is a region in southwestern Mali bordering Mauritania, positioned along important movement corridors between western Mali and the Sahel. The region is largely rural and semi-arid, with livelihoods centered on agriculture, pastoralism, and trade. Its border location and long road networks make it strategically relevant for both commerce and security.

Jihadism in Nara

Nara has generally experienced lower levels of jihadist violence than Mali’s central and northern hotspots, but it remains exposed to risks linked to cross-border movement and insecurity along transport routes. As militant groups expand influence and exploit weak governance in peripheral areas, regions like Nara can face episodic attacks and heightened threat perceptions even when baseline violence remains comparatively limited.

For humanitarian actors, risk in Nara often depends heavily on route security and current incident trends, especially when operating near the border or traveling between major towns.

Violence Exposure Index

Nara has a Violence Exposure Index (VEI) of 33 out of 100 indicating low to moderate relative exposure to jihadist violence. VEI is calculated using total terrorist attacks and deaths from those attacks recorded in the Global Terrorism Database (GTD) since 2000. Attacks and deaths after 2015 are given more weight [1].
