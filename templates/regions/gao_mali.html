Gao, Mali

VEI: 40

Introduction

Gao is a major city and region in northeastern Mali, situated along the Niger River and historically serving as a key commercial and administrative center of the Sahel. Its geographic position near the borders with Niger and Burkina Faso has made it a strategic hub for trade, migration, and military movement for centuries.

Jihadism in Gao

Gao has been one of the epicenters of jihadist and insurgent violence in Mali since the collapse of state authority in northern Mali in 2012. Armed groups affiliated with al-Qaeda and the Islamic State have used the region as a base for operations, exploiting porous borders, limited state presence, and long-standing local grievances.

Since 2012, Gao has experienced sustained militant activity including attacks on Malian security forces, international peacekeepers, and civilians. The region remains a critical operational zone for jihadist groups, with violence fluctuating in intensity but persisting over time.

Violence Exposure Index

Gao has a Violence Exposure Index (VEI) of 40 out of 100, indicating a medium relative exposure to jihadist violence. VEI is calculated using the frequency and lethality of terrorist attacks recorded in the Global Terrorism Database (GTD) since 2000, with greater weight assigned to incidents occurring after 2015.

It is important to note that prior to 2016, attacks in what is now the Ménaka Region were recorded under Gao, as Ménaka did not yet exist as a separate administrative region in the GTD [1]. As a result, historical violence figures for Gao reflect militant activity across a broader geographic area than the modern Gao Region alone.
