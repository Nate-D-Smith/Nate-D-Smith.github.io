Bamako Mali
Violence Intensity Index (VII): 5

Introduction

Bamako is the capital city of Mali and the region around the city. It is also the most important economic region for Mali sitting on the Niger River which connects it to most of Mali and the ocean. Bamako has held political significance since 1908 when the French named it the capitol of French Sudan. Unlike most of the country, Bamako has seen significant development in recent years mostly due to its political power and connections to surrounding countries.

Jihadism in Bamako

Bamako was the target of many attacks during the Islamic Insurgency in the Sahel in the early 2010s and has continued to see attacks since the founding of JNIM in 2017. Bamako is currently being blocked from the rest of the world by JNIM in an effort to pressure the government to cede power. Food and gasoline are boing denied to the city as JNIM attacks any supply convoys en route to Bamako from surrounding countries. In a matter of a few weeks it is very possible that we could see yet another country fall to an affiliate of al-Qaeda.
