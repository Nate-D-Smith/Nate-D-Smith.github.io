Tombouctou, Mali

VEI:

Introduction

Tombouctou is one of Mali’s most historically significant regions, known for its cultural heritage and strategic position at the edge of the Sahara. Despite its symbolic importance, the region faces profound economic and governance challenges, particularly in rural and desert areas. Since 2012, Tombouctou has been deeply affected by armed conflict and political instability.

Jihadism in Tombouctou

Jihadist activity in Tombouctou has been persistent and severe, making it one of the most violence-exposed regions in Mali. Militant groups have carried out attacks against civilians, military forces, and local authorities while leveraging the region’s vast terrain for mobility and concealment. Tombouctou continues to function as both a frontline and support zone for jihadist operations, reflecting sustained insurgent pressure and limited state control.

Violence Exposure Index

Tombouctou has a Violence Exposure Index (VEI) of 47 out of 100 indicating high relative risk of violence. VEI is our score calculated using total terrorist attacks and deaths from those attacks since 2000. Attacks and deaths after 2015 are given more weight. With 292 deaths from 132 attacks since 2015 [1], Tombouctou has heavy jihadist militant action.
