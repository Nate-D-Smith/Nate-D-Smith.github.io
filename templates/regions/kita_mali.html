Kita, Mali

VEI: 9

Introduction

Kita is a region in western Mali located between Kayes and Koulikoro, characterized by a mix of savannah and agricultural land. The region serves as an important local center for farming, trade, and transport, with road connections linking it to Bamako and neighboring regions.

Historically, Kita has experienced lower levels of political violence compared to northern and central Mali. Its relative distance from major conflict zones has contributed to a more stable security environment over much of the past two decades.

Jihadism in Kita

Jihadist militant activity in Kita has been limited when compared to regions such as Mopti, Gao, or Kidal. However, the broader deterioration of security in Mali has raised concerns about potential southward and westward spillover over time.

For humanitarian and development actors, Kita is generally considered a lower-risk operating environment, though continued monitoring of road security and regional trends remains important given Mali’s evolving conflict dynamics.

Violence Exposure Index

Kita has a Violence Exposure Index (VEI) of 9 out of 100, indicating very low relative exposure to jihadist violence. VEI is calculated using total terrorist attacks and deaths recorded in the Global Terrorism Database (GTD) since 2000, with incidents after 2015 receiving greater weight [1].
