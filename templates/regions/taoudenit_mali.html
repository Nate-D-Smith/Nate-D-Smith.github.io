Taoudenit, Mali

VEI:

Introduction

Taoudénit is a vast, sparsely populated region in northern Mali dominated by desert terrain and minimal infrastructure. The region has very few population centers and limited state presence, functioning more as a transit and logistical space than a conventional administrative region. Its remoteness has historically limited economic development while increasing strategic importance for non-state armed actors.

Jihadism in Taoudenit

Although Taoudénit records relatively few attacks in absolute terms, it remains a high-risk operational environment for jihadist groups. The region serves as a rear base and transit zone for militant networks, offering concealment, freedom of movement, and limited interference from state forces. Violence in Taoudénit is often underreported and does not typically involve civilian targets, masking the region’s strategic role in sustaining jihadist operations across northern Mali.

Violence Exposure Index

Taoudenit has a Violence Exposure Index (VEI) of out of 100 indicating relative risk of violence. VEI is our score calculated using total terrorist attacks and deaths from those attacks since 2000. Attacks and deaths after 2015 are given more weight. With deaths from attacks since 2015 [1], Taoudenit has jihadist militant action.
