Sikasso, Mali

VEI:

Introduction

Sikasso is Mali’s southernmost region and one of its most economically productive areas, known for agriculture, trade, and cross-border commerce with Côte d’Ivoire and Burkina Faso. The region has long been considered one of Mali’s most stable and prosperous, with strong social cohesion and relatively effective local governance. Its geographic distance from Mali’s primary conflict zones has helped preserve this stability.

Jihadism in Sikasso

Jihadist militant activity in Sikasso has been extremely limited. While the broader Sahelian insurgency has raised concerns about potential southward expansion, Sikasso has largely avoided attacks and sustained militant presence. Security incidents in the region are rare and typically linked to regional instability rather than local insurgent organization, making Sikasso one of the least affected regions in Mali.

Violence Exposure Index

Sikasso has a Violence Exposure Index (VEI) of out of 100 indicating relative risk of violence. VEI is our score calculated using total terrorist attacks and deaths from those attacks since 2000. Attacks and deaths after 2015 are given more weight. With deaths from attacks since 2015 [1], Sikasso has jihadist militant action.
