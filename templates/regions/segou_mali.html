Segou, Mali

Introduction

Ségou is a central region of Mali situated along the Niger River and holds significant agricultural, economic, and demographic importance. Its position as a connective corridor between southern Mali and the conflict-affected center has made it increasingly exposed to regional insecurity. Over the past decade, Ségou has transitioned from a relatively stable region to one facing mounting security challenges.

Jihadism in Segou

Jihadist violence in Ségou has increased notably since the mid-2010s as militant groups expanded southward from Mopti. The region has experienced attacks targeting military positions, local officials, and civilians, particularly in rural areas where state presence is limited. Ségou now represents a key zone of expansion for jihadist groups seeking to destabilize central Mali, making it one of the more exposed regions outside the far north.
