Mopti, Mali

VEI: 76

Introduction

Mopti is a central region of Mali situated at the crossroads between northern and southern Mali, centered around the Inland Niger Delta. The region is ethnically diverse and heavily dependent on agriculture, fishing, and pastoralism, making it both economically important and socially complex.

Its geographic position has long made Mopti a point of interaction between different communities and livelihood systems, but it has also made the region particularly vulnerable to insecurity and conflict spillover.

Jihadism in Mopti

Mopti has been one of the most severely affected regions in Mali’s jihadist insurgency since 2015. The region has served as a major area of operations for Jama’at Nusrat al-Islam wal-Muslimin (JNIM), which has exploited local grievances, intercommunal tensions, and limited state presence.

Violence in Mopti has included attacks on civilians, local militias, security forces, and humanitarian actors, as well as large-scale displacement. The region is widely regarded as a central battleground in Mali’s conflict due to its strategic importance and sustained levels of militant activity.

Violence Exposure Index

Mopti has a Violence Exposure Index (VEI) of 76 out of 100, indicating high relative exposure to jihadist violence. VEI is calculated using total terrorist attacks and deaths recorded in the Global Terrorism Database (GTD) since 2000, with incidents occurring after 2015 given greater weight [1].

Due to its role as a central transit and population hub, violence in Mopti has had outsized humanitarian consequences, contributing significantly to displacement and food insecurity across Mali.
