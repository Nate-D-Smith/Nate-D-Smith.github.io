Kayes, Mali

VEI: 18

Introduction

Kayes is the westernmost region of Mali and a key transport and trade corridor linking Mali to Senegal and Mauritania. The regional capital, also named Kayes, sits along the Senegal River and has long served as a strategic hub for migration, commerce, and regional logistics. Compared to northern and central Mali, Kayes has historically experienced lower levels of jihadist violence, but shifting insecurity elsewhere in the country can still affect the region through transit routes and cross-border dynamics.

Jihadism in Kayes

Kayes is not typically considered an epicenter of militant activity in Mali. However, humanitarian travel and operations in the region can still be influenced by Mali’s broader security situation, including route-level risks, opportunistic violence, and the potential for spillover from areas with higher militant presence. For many organizations, Kayes functions as a transit and staging region, making road security and real-time incident monitoring especially important.

Violence Exposure Index

Kayes has a Violence Exposure Index (VEI) of 18 out of 100 indicating low relative exposure to jihadist violence. VEI is calculated using total terrorist attacks and deaths from those attacks recorded in the Global Terrorism Database (GTD) since 2000. Attacks and deaths after 2015 are given more weight [1].
