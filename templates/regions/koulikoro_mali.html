Koulikoro, Mali

VEI: 22

Introduction

Koulikoro is a central-western region of Mali located directly northeast of Bamako. The region plays an important logistical and administrative role due to its proximity to the capital and its position along major road and river corridors connecting southern and central Mali.

The region includes both urban centers and extensive rural areas, with livelihoods centered on agriculture, trade, and transport. Koulikoro’s closeness to Bamako makes it strategically significant despite historically lower levels of militant violence compared to northern Mali.

Jihadism in Koulikoro

While Koulikoro has not traditionally been a primary hub of jihadist activity, it has experienced increasing security pressures in recent years as militant groups expand their operational reach southward from central Mali. Attacks in peripheral areas and along transport routes have raised concerns about the gradual spread of insecurity.

For humanitarian organizations, Koulikoro is often traversed en route to other regions, making route security and situational awareness particularly important even when overall violence levels remain comparatively low.

Violence Exposure Index

Koulikoro has a Violence Exposure Index (VEI) of 22 out of 100, indicating low relative exposure to jihadist violence. VEI is calculated using total terrorist attacks and deaths recorded in the Global Terrorism Database (GTD) since 2000, with incidents occurring after 2015 weighted more heavily [1].
