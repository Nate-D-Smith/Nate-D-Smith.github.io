Jama'at Nasr al-Islam wal-Muslimin (JNIM)

Introduction and foundation

Jama'at Nasr al-Islam wal-Muslimin (JNIM) is a Salafi Jihadist militant organisation operating in the Sahel region of sub-Saharan Africa. It was created in 2017 as a merger of 4 salafi-jihadist organizations in the sahel (including AQIM) and has since opperated as a sub-group of al-Qaeda.

The ranks of JNIM total 6,000 troops mainly from the Tuareg and Fulani ethnic groups in Mali. It operates in both Arabic and French but curent leaders prefer Arabic. The curent leader and founder of JNIM is Iyad Ag Ghaly, a Tuareg militant who has fought in various rebellions since the 1980s. He was the founder and leader of the former militant group Ansar Dine and became the leader of JNIM upon it's creation.

Ideology and methods

JNIM follows a strict version of salafism which is a fundamentalist movement within Sunni Islam that emphasises the purity of original islamic practices and rejects later innovations and developments.It also emphasises the supremacy of the Quran over all other interpretations of the message of the prophet Muhammad.

Though JNIM is one of the dealiest terrorist groups om the world,it often acts as a moderate option compared to Islamic State in the Greater Sahara (ISGS). While ISGS attacks civilians to cause fear, JNIM practices a "hearts and minds" approach acting as a stablizing legal force in areas it controls. Most of the recent attacks that JNIM has committed have been against government assets in an effort to topple it and gain power.
