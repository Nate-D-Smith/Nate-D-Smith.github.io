Mali

Introduction

Mali is a country in the Sahel that has thousands of years of legendary history ranging from the conquests of the Mali empire to the conquests of Islam and from the glorious reign of history's richest man Mansa Musa to the oppressive reign of post colonial military juntas. Over it's history, Mali has served as a melting pot of ethnic groups, religions, languages, philosophies, and ideologies. The combination of these things has created one of the most interesting and chaotic cultures of our day.

Mali has been at the heart of the Sahel's security crisis for more than a decade. What began as a local rebellion in 2012 has evolved into a complex conflict involving jihadist groups, ethnic militias, international forces, and state actors. Today, Mali is the most active theater of jihadist violence in the Sahel and the birthplace of several groups that now operate across West Africa.

Militant Groups and Violence

The primary among these is Jama'at Nasr al-Islam wal-Muslimin or JNIM which is a Jihadist militant group born out of the Northern Tuareg rebellions and currently allied with al-Qaeda. It has ravaged the Northern regions of Mali since 2017 with bombings, executions, theft, and armed raids. In spite of this violence JNIM has earned the respect and loyalty of many groups in Mali's border regions, especially among ethnic Turareg and Fulani tribes who have been historically oppressed by the state. It concentrates most of it's attacks on state government and military assets in order to delegitimize the state. JNIM also has conducted hundreds of attacks against the Islamic State Sahel Province (ISSP) with whom it competes for control of the tri-border region of Mali, Niger, and Burkina Faso. Mali civialians often get caught up in the crossfire as groups frequently massacre villages who are seen as friendly to the ennemy.

Mali has long been riddled by violence. Mali was invaded and controled by France as part of Afrique Occidentale Francaise from 1893 until its independence in 1960. French colonial rule involved harsh administration including forced labor, heavy taxation, and forced military recruitment.
